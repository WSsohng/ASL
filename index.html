Analytical Spectroscopy

Chemical Analysis

ASL develops quantitative and interpretable analytical workflows for complex chemical systems. We combine spectroscopy, signal preprocessing, and chemometrics to solve translational problems in bio, materials, and environmental applications.

Publication Archive Gallery Archive

Laboratory Scope

Measurement, Modeling, Validation.

We execute end-to-end analytical pipelines from instrument setup and spectral acquisition to model validation. Our objective is robust, reproducible quantitation under real-world measurement variability.

Research Focus

Research Themes

Near-Infrared Spectroscopy

01 / 05

We develop NIR methods for rapid and non-destructive quantitation of composition and quality indices in complex samples. Calibration transfer and robustness testing are integrated for field-ready deployment.

Raman Spectroscopy

02 / 05

Transmission and wide-area Raman schemes are designed to improve selectivity and throughput in heterogeneous matrices. Applications include food analytics, bio-samples, and process streams.

Surface-Enhanced Raman (SERS)

03 / 05

Reproducible and reusable SERS substrates are engineered for trace-level detection of biomarkers and environmental targets. We focus on signal stability, enhancement factors, and assay repeatability.

Chemometrics & Data Fusion

04 / 05

Multivariate pipelines including PCA, PLS, PLS-DA, and SVM are used to maximize predictive performance. Standardized preprocessing and validation protocols support reproducible model deployment.

Electrochemical Analysis

05 / 05

Electrochemical interfaces are integrated with optical sensing to improve sensitivity and selectivity for bioanalytical targets. The platform supports translation from lab-scale assays to practical screening.

Professor

Hoeil Chung

Professor | Analytical Chemistry | Hanyang University

Prof. Hoeil Chung leads ASL in molecular spectroscopy and chemometrics for quantitative chemical analysis, with emphasis on Raman/SERS and near-infrared sensing workflows.

  • B.S. Chemistry, Hanyang University (1989)
  • Ph.D. Chemistry, University of Iowa (1994)
  • Industrial R&D at SK Energy: NIR online process monitoring systems
  • Professor at Hanyang University since 2002
  • 200+ peer-reviewed publications; ICNIRS PAC Elected Member (2023-2027)
